# RGH Layer 0 (Entropic Substrate Hypothesis): Simulation Findings

**Status: New hypothesis. Core result (open vs. closed system) replicated cleanly across seeds and sharpened into a precise, mechanistically-grounded claim. Noise source tested and resolved: system openness matters, origin of the fluctuation does not (qualitatively robust across seeds and budgets; effect magnitude is calibration-dependent in ways not yet fully understood). Extended to Persistence: a genuine asymmetry found — Distinction requires dissipation outright, Persistence-by-attrition does not, and the sharpening effect dissipation does provide is a threshold, not a gradient. The instability boundary is now mapped precisely as a high-sensitivity transitional band, not a single point. Summarized in the main paper, Section 4.1; full detail here.**

---

## 1. Hypothesis under test

The main paper's central conclusion is that Distinction, Transformation, and Persistence describe a recurring pattern in complex systems, but are not themselves a sufficient generative engine — every real effect found required some further, specific, contingent mechanism underneath. "Layer 0" is a candidate for what that underlying mechanism might be, at least in part: the proposal that genuine non-equilibrium thermodynamic flux (real, ongoing dissipation) is what actually drives Transformation, sieves Persistence, and may be required for Distinction's boundary formation in the first place.

This document tests the narrowest, most direct piece of that claim: **does the causal-insulation result already confirmed for Axiom 1 (Distinction) actually require genuine dissipative flux, or does any comparable fluctuation produce the same result regardless of whether the system is thermodynamically open or closed?**

## 2. Method

**Substrate**: the validated Gray-Scott reaction-diffusion system used for the original Axiom 1 result (Du=0.16, Dv=0.08), with the same ignition protocol (spatially-correlated noise, amplitude 0.10, correlation length 1.0).

**Core comparison**: Condition A (dissipative, F=0.035, k=0.065 — the validated "spots" regime) against Condition B (non-dissipative, F=0, k=0 — identical diffusion and reaction nonlinearity, but thermodynamically closed rather than open). A third condition, C, is the raw noise field before any dynamics run, for reference.

**Metric**: spatial variance of the field over time. An initial attempt to reuse the causal-insulation mutual-information test (validated for the original Axiom 1 result) on Condition B was found to produce a genuine estimator artifact — Condition B's field, once collapsed, undergoes smooth monotonic relaxation rather than genuine dynamic activity, which inflates mutual information under a histogram-based estimator without reflecting any real structure. This is documented as a real methodological boundary, not glossed over: the mutual-information test does not transfer meaningfully to a system in this regime, and spatial variance is the metric relied on throughout this document instead.

**A second substrate for the Persistence extension (Section 3.5)**: the validated energy-conservation Game-of-Life system (`stage1_salvageability_memory.py`, `stage6_component_memory.py`, `stage8_differential_survival.py`, `vectorized_signature.py` — reused directly from the Axioms 2&3 folder), where metabolic cost plays the role Gray-Scott's F, k play for Distinction. Note: this folder's own `stage6_noise_source_control.py` (for the Distinction noise-source test) and the reused `stage6_component_memory.py` (for the Persistence test) share a numeric prefix by coincidence of when each was written, not because they're related — they serve entirely different tests on different substrates.

## 3. Results

### 3.1 Core comparison, replicated across 15 seeds

Condition A sustains real, growing structure (mean final V-field standard deviation 0.114, seed-to-seed variation tiny — pstdev 0.0005). Condition B collapses almost completely (mean final V-std 0.0009, pstdev 0.0002) — more uniform than the raw noise baseline it started from. Every one of 15 independent seeds showed A greater than B; the paired difference (0.113) is enormous relative to the seed-to-seed noise in either condition (paired t = 769.5). This is as clean a replication as any result in this project.

### 3.2 A sharper picture: dissipation is necessary but not sufficient

A follow-up swept the dissipation strength between the two extremes (scaling F and k together from 0 to full strength) to see whether structure degrades gradually or collapses at a sharp threshold. The result revealed three distinct regimes, not a simple two-state comparison:

- **No dissipation at all (scale 0)**: the field saturates to a high, uniform value (V≈0.95) — an unconstrained reaction runaway, since nothing removes V once produced.
- **Partial dissipation (scales 0.05 through 0.80)**: genuine, ongoing, non-zero dissipative throughput is present — F and k are both nonzero — yet the field still collapses to a uniform, non-trivial fixed point (V settling around 0.34 at scale 0.10, for example). Every one of these intermediate scales produced essentially zero spatial variance, with zero seed-to-seed variation, indicating a real, stable equilibrium rather than noise.
- **Sufficient dissipation (scales above roughly 0.80–0.85)**: real spatial structure emerges and grows smoothly with increasing scale, from V-std ≈ 0.075 at scale 0.85 up to ≈ 0.117 at scale 0.97, matching the full-strength result.

This means dissipation being present is not, by itself, sufficient for structure to form. A dissipative system can still relax to a uniform state if that state happens to be dynamically stable against spatial perturbation. What matters is whether the system's dissipative regime crosses into genuine spatial instability (a Turing bifurcation) — a real, sharp threshold in parameter space, not a smooth function of "how much" dissipation is present.

### 3.3 Causal insulation confirmed within the partial-dissipation regime, not just at full strength

The original causal-insulation test was only ever run at full dissipation strength. A follow-up applied the identical, validated mutual-information methodology at scale 0.90 — comfortably inside the newly-identified Turing-unstable band, but well short of full strength. The result matched: Interior MI (0.356) exceeded Boundary MI (0.162), a gap of +0.194 — nearly identical in size to the full-strength gap (+0.199). Fewer and smaller spots formed at this reduced strength (14 versus 61), consistent with weaker overall structure, but the causal-insulation signature itself — the thing Axiom 1 actually claims — held throughout. This closes the gap noted in the previous version of this document: partial dissipation, provided it is on the unstable side of the threshold, produces the same qualitative signature as full dissipation, not merely similar variance.

### 3.4 Noise source: substantial effect on aggregate structure, no effect on causal insulation itself

Every test above used noise only as a one-time initial perturbation, then let the system evolve deterministically. A separate question — flagged but untested in the earlier version of this document — is whether the *noise itself* needs to be dissipation-sourced, or whether any ongoing fluctuation suffices as long as the system remains thermodynamically open. This was tested directly: ongoing per-step noise was injected throughout the run (not just at ignition), on the same open, dissipative system (F=0.035, k=0.065, unchanged), comparing two sources at matched average amplitude — noise scaling with the local reaction rate (U·V², mimicking real molecular fluctuation, which physically scales with reaction throughput) against noise injected uniformly across the grid regardless of local activity.

The two sources produced substantially different aggregate outcomes. Across a sweep of noise budgets (0.01 to 0.1), dissipation-sourced noise consistently produced a much lower mean field level (e.g., 0.026 at budget 0.05) than dissipation-independent noise (0.193 at the same budget) — a 5- to 20-fold difference, holding across every budget tested. The mechanism is direct: dissipation-sourced noise is proportional to local reaction rate, which is near-zero in quiet background regions, so those regions are barely perturbed and stay quiet. Dissipation-independent noise perturbs the entire grid uniformly, including quiet regions — and because the underlying reaction is autocatalytic, uniformly kicking previously-quiet background triggers much more widespread secondary ignition, producing a far higher overall field level and more numerous spots (507 versus 122 at matched budget).

Despite this large difference in aggregate behavior, the causal-insulation signature itself was checked directly and found intact in both conditions: Interior MI exceeded Boundary MI by a similar margin under both dissipation-sourced noise (+0.033) and dissipation-independent noise (+0.027). (Background MI was elevated in both conditions, consistent with the same low-variance estimator artifact already documented for the original Axiom 1 result — this does not affect the Interior-versus-Boundary comparison, which is the test that actually bears on the causal-insulation claim.)

### 3.5 Extended to Persistence: a real asymmetry between axioms

Distinction requires genuine dissipative flux — every test above confirms that removing it destroys structure outright. This section asks the analogous question for Persistence: does the differential-survival drift toward high-persistence-confidence configurations (the validated Axioms 2&3 finding, in which population composition drifts toward blocks and gliders purely through ordinary attrition) also require real dissipation, or does it emerge from the discrete transformation rule alone?

The energy-CA substrate's direct analog to Gray-Scott's F, k is metabolic cost — the ongoing energy drain a cell pays just to keep existing. This was tested directly: the exact validated differential-survival protocol, same held-out seeds, same independently-built confidence memory, compared at metabolic cost 0.04 (dissipative, the validated baseline) against metabolic cost 0 (non-dissipative — cells die only via Game of Life's own neighbor-count rule, with no energetic cost of persisting at all).

At the original 2,000-step duration, both conditions showed the same qualitative drift — mean confidence rising from a random-soup baseline of 0.405 toward higher values in both cases — but the non-dissipative condition had not yet reached a stable plateau. Extending the run to 6,000 steps resolved this: **both conditions reach genuine, hard equilibria**, confidence and population locked to an exact, unchanging value for the remainder of the run in both cases. The dissipative condition settles by step ~1,550 at confidence 0.657, population 15.3. The non-dissipative condition settles later, by step ~3,050, at confidence 0.643, population 77.0 — a similar final confidence level (within about 2%), reached more slowly and at a substantially larger surviving population (roughly 5×).

**Unlike Distinction, Persistence-by-attrition does not require dissipation.** The phenomenon is a property of the discrete transformation rule's own logic — unstable configurations die under Game of Life's rule regardless of any energetic cost layered on top. Metabolic cost is not necessary for the drift to occur, but it does sharpen and accelerate it: it adds a real, additional culling pressure on top of the rule's own selection, reaching a similar qualitative outcome faster and at a sparser, more tightly-selected population.

### 3.6 The instability boundary, mapped precisely: a sensitive band, not a single point

The original dissipation sweep bracketed the Distinction instability threshold between scales 0.80 and 0.85. A finer sweep (0.78 to 0.87, 10 seeds per point) mapped this precisely, and found something more specific than a single sharp transition: scales 0.80–0.82 show large seed-to-seed variance (at scale 0.80, standard deviation across seeds is nearly as large as the mean itself), while 0.78–0.79 are uniformly near-zero and 0.83 onward is uniformly consistent and smoothly increasing.

This was checked directly rather than assumed to be simple noise: individual seed outcomes at scale 0.81 were inspected and found to form a continuous spread (0.013 to 0.070) with no gap separating distinct groups — not clean bistability (two discrete outcomes), but a genuine high-sensitivity transitional band, where the eventual structure strength depends continuously and sensitively on the specific noise realization. Below this band, every seed reliably stays quiet; above it, every seed reliably and smoothly converges. Within it, small differences in initial conditions produce a wide, continuous range of outcomes — a real, specific feature of the transition's shape, not an artifact of insufficient sampling.

### 3.7 Noise-source result replicated across seeds and budgets, with an honest wrinkle

The original noise-source comparison (Section 3.4) rested on a single seed at one calibration. This was replicated across 6 seeds at the original budget (0.05) and 5 seeds at a second budget (0.03). The qualitative result holds firmly: every single seed, in both conditions and at both budgets, shows a positive Interior-Boundary gap — causal insulation is real and consistent regardless of noise source, confirming the core conclusion is not an artifact of one lucky run.

The magnitudes, however, are not identical across budgets, and this is worth reporting rather than averaging away. At budget 0.05, sourced and independent noise produce similar gap sizes (0.029 versus 0.026). At budget 0.03, the ordering reverses in magnitude: independent noise produces a much larger, more consistent gap (0.128, tight across seeds) than sourced noise (0.027, similar to before). The qualitative claim — both sources produce real causal insulation — replicates robustly. The specific size of the effect depends on calibration in ways not yet fully characterized, and should not be treated as a fixed constant.

### 3.8 The Persistence dissipation-sharpening effect is a threshold, not a gradient

Section 3.5 compared only two metabolic-cost values (0.04 and 0). A sweep across intermediate values (0, 0.01, 0.02, 0.03, 0.04) on the same protocol found the effect is not a smooth gradient: costs 0.00, 0.01, and 0.02 produced **identical** outcomes (confidence 0.6433, population 77.0, settling at the same step, for every value) — not approximately similar, but byte-for-byte identical population trajectories. This was verified directly rather than assumed to be a bug: energy values genuinely differ between these conditions (mean energy 0.832 at cost 0 versus 0.726 at cost 0.02, confirmed after 50 steps), but which specific cells live or die does not change at all, because survival depends only on energy staying positive, and background inflow and birth-cost mechanics keep energy comfortably positive at these low costs regardless of the small additional drain.

Only at cost 0.03 does a partial shift appear (confidence 0.639, population 73.6), and the full effect described in Section 3.5 requires cost 0.04. This is the same "empirically inert parameter" phenomenon documented earlier in this project's Exploitation/Adaptation calibration work, now independently reconfirmed in a completely different context: metabolic cost is a genuine threshold effect on this substrate, not a dial that sharpens Persistence's selection pressure smoothly and continuously.

## 4. What this establishes

The core finding replicates cleanly and dramatically: removing dissipation entirely, while holding everything else constant, destroys structure rather than merely weakening it. The follow-up sweep sharpens this into a more precise and more defensible claim than "dissipation is required": **genuine non-equilibrium flux is necessary for this structure to form, but it must also put the system into a regime where its own dissipative equilibrium is unstable to perturbation — dissipation alone, without that instability, produces a different, merely uniform, dissipative state.** This is consistent with, and a real instance of, established dissipative-structure theory (Prigogine), which has always required both far-from-equilibrium driving and an instability mechanism together, not throughput in isolation.

The noise-source question is now resolved in favor of the weaker of the two candidate claims: **what matters is that the system is open and driven, not where the fluctuation specifically originates.** Noise source has a real, substantial effect on aggregate field statistics (overall activity level, number and density of structures), but the specific causal-insulation signature that defines genuine Distinction in this framework forms comparably under both dissipation-sourced and dissipation-independent noise, given the same open, dissipative system underneath.

**Extending this line to Persistence reveals that Layer 0's dissipation requirement is not uniform across the axioms.** Distinction cannot occur without genuine thermodynamic flux — remove it, and the phenomenon vanishes outright. Persistence-by-attrition can occur without it — the phenomenon is intrinsic to the transformation rule itself, with dissipation acting as an accelerant and intensifier rather than a precondition. This is a real, specific, and useful finding about what Layer 0 actually governs: not a single, uniform substrate requirement underlying every axiom equally, but a requirement whose necessity differs by which axiom is being asked about.

## 5. What this does NOT establish

- **This tests two of the three axioms.** Distinction requires dissipation; Persistence-by-attrition does not, though dissipation sharpens it, in a threshold rather than gradient manner. Transformation was not tested the same way, and for a specific reason worth stating plainly: Transformation, as this framework defines it, is a framework choice (a fixed local rule), not a phenomenon that occurs or fails to occur depending on energetic conditions — there is no "Transformation without a rule" condition to compare against, the way there was a meaningful "no structure" versus "structure" comparison for Distinction and Persistence. Whether the *computational richness* of a transformation rule (its capacity for complex, irreducible dynamics) depends on dissipation is a different, harder, and not-yet-well-defined question, distinct from what this document tests.
- **One substrate (Gray-Scott for Distinction, energy-CA for Persistence), one ignition/seeding protocol each.** The dissipation-scaling sweep held F:k ratio fixed while scaling both together; a different scaling path through parameter space might reveal a different-shaped boundary.
- **The partial-regime causal-insulation confirmation (Section 3.3) rests on a single seed**, unlike the full-strength result, which was validated with bootstrap resampling across multiple samples.
- **The noise-source effect's magnitude is calibration-dependent in ways not yet fully characterized** (Section 3.7): the qualitative result (positive gap under both sources) is now confirmed across seeds and two budgets, but why the relative size of sourced versus independent gaps reverses between budgets 0.03 and 0.05 is not yet understood mechanistically.
- **The background-MI artifact, already flagged for the original Axiom 1 result, reappears here** and is more pronounced under dissipation-sourced noise specifically (since it leaves background regions especially quiet) — this affects only the Background comparison, not the Interior-versus-Boundary test the conclusion rests on.
- **The high-sensitivity transitional band (Section 3.6) is characterized at one substrate, one noise-correlation setting.** Whether its width and location generalize to other ignition conditions is untested.
- **This is not yet part of the main paper's supporting appendix code**, though its findings are summarized in the main text, Section 4.1.

## 6. Suggested next steps

1. If Transformation is to be tested at all, reframe the question first: not "does Transformation require dissipation" (a category error, given Transformation is definitionally a fixed rule), but whether a rule's *computational class* — its capacity for complex, irreducible dynamics rather than simple fixed-point or periodic behavior — depends on energetic conditions. This would need a genuinely different experimental design than anything built for Distinction or Persistence.
2. Investigate mechanistically why the noise-source gap magnitude reverses between budgets 0.03 and 0.05, rather than simply reporting both as confirming instances of the same qualitative claim.
3. Test whether the high-sensitivity transitional band (Section 3.6) narrows, widens, or shifts under different ignition or correlation-length conditions.
