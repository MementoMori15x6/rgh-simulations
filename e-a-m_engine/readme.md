## The Recursive Specification of the $E \rightarrow A \rightarrow M$ Engine

In a resource-constrained computational universe, this loop is a localized transformation function ($f_{RGH}$) that processes structural interactions. It operates at three nested levels:

```text
                  [ STAGE 1: EXPLOITATION ]
                 Open-loop resource extraction.
             High entropy, unsustainable long-term.
                           │
                           ▼
                  [ STAGE 2: ADAPTATION ]
            Stress-induced search for stability.
           Rule-mutation triggered by energy drop.
                           │
                           ▼
                  [ STAGE 3: MUTUALISM ]
         *Only* when closed-loop systems intersect.
        Defection is structurally encrypted into a
               permanent positive-sum loop.

```

### 1. Exploitation: The Open-Loop Baseline

Exploitation is the default state when two asymmetric structures intersect on the grid. It is an **open-loop extraction**. Structure $A$ pulls energy from Structure $B$.

* *Why it can't sustain itself:* It relies on a continuous, uninsulated gradient. Eventually, it burns out its host/neighbor, destroying its own local resource base. It creates an unmitigated entropic drag.

### 2. Adaptation: The Feedback Trigger

Adaptation is not a conscious choice; it is the **computational response to the boundary collapse caused by exploitation**. When the energy depletion from Stage 1 threatens the persistence of the structure, the cell or neighborhood is forced to shift its rule space. It is the transition mechanism—the search protocol looking for a way to break the downward spiral.

### 3. Mutualism: The Closed-Loop Attractor

This is the heart of your realization. Mutualism cannot happen between raw, unformed, open-ended chaos. **It requires the intersection of two closed-loop, self-replicating systems.**

* **What a "closed loop" means here:** An entity that already possesses a self-sustaining internal rule cycle (like the specific birth/survival patterns in GoL, or a metabolic cycle).
* **The Locking Mechanism:** When two closed loops exploit each other, they don't just destroy pixels—they destabilize each other's *internal cycles*. The only way for either to survive the adaptive shock is to intertwine their loops so that the output of $A$'s internal cycle directly satisfies the input requirement of $B$'s internal cycle, and vice versa.

The loop closes. The exploitation doesn't vanish; it is **permanently captured and encrypted inside a positive-sum boundary.**
